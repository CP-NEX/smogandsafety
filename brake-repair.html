To maintain air quality and reduce the impact of vehicle emissions on smog levels, the state mandates smog checks for most vehicles every two years during the registration renewal process. Vehicles brought in from out of state are also required to undergo a smog test, except for cars manufactured prior to 1975, which are exempt.

Smog and Safety Inspection is a certified STAR smog test station, which means our testing procedures adhere to rigorous performance standards set by Smog and Safety Inspection. Our skilled technicians conduct smog tests for both foreign and domestic vehicles. If your vehicle doesn't pass, we provide professional repair services to ensure it passes the next test.

In fact, Smog and Safety Inspection has earned such a strong reputation for its smog check services and repairs that we are the preferred smog check station in San Diego for UPS and FedEx.

Warning Signs Of Brake Issues

Certain components of your brake system are engineered to signal when it's time for maintenance or repair, like brake pads squealing as they wear down excessively. However, it's essential to remain vigilant for these additional warning signs that suggest a brake system inspection is required:

  • Grinding Sound When You Apply The Brakes.
  • Brake Pedal Pulsates Or Feels Spongy.
  • Vehicle Vibrates When Braking.
  • Vehicle Takes Longer Than Usual To Come To A Stop.
  • Brake Warning Light Comes On While Driving.

If you observe any of these warning signs or experience other unusual sounds or sensations related to your vehicle's brake system, we strongly recommend scheduling an inspection with Smog and Safety Inspection without delay.
