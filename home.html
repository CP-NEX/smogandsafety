Frequently Asked Questions

Who Needs Smog Inspection?

California requires all gasoline and hybrid vehicles 1976 and newer to undergo smog inspection. Additionally, all 1998 and newer diesel engine vehicles are required to obtain smog certification, as well as.

Depending on your zip code you may be required to undergo a smog inspection every other year or just when you transfer ownership of the vehicle. If your vehicle is eight years old or newer, you are exempt from smog certification.

Ask Us Anything

Does My Vehicle Qualify for a Smog Exemption?

↓
Your vehicle is exempt from smog inspections if it falls into one of the following categories: Gasoline-powered, 1975 year model or older Diesel-powered manufactured before 1998 or with a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) over 14,000 lbs Electric Natural gas-powered with a GVWR over 14,000 lbs Motorcycle Trailer

When is a brake and lamp inspection needed?

↓
A brake and lamp inspection is commonly required by the California DMV for revived salvage title vehicles, vehicles being imported from out of state, or when resolving a defect citation issued by the Highway Patrol.

What are common signs of wheel alignment problems?

↓
Common signs include the vehicle pulling strongly to one side, uneven or rapid tire wear, steering wheel being crooked or off-center when driving straight, and squealing tires.

What are signs a vehicle needs brake repair?

↓
Warning signs include grinding or squealing noises under braking, a spongy or soft brake pedal, vibration or pulsing in the steering wheel, and the brake dashboard warning light coming on.

Does the shop handle inspection-related repairs?

↓
Yes, we are a licensed test-and-repair station. If your vehicle fails a smog test, we can diagnose and complete the necessary repairs in-house.
