Complete Wheel Alignment Service

While everyday wear and tear often lead to wheel misalignment, it can also be caused by minor factors like low tire pressure or more significant issues like undisclosed suspension damage. At Smog and Safety Inspection, our certified technicians are skilled in examining all alignment-related components to rule out any underlying mechanical or maintenance concerns. Our comprehensive wheel alignment service encompasses:

  • Accurate Measurement Of Three Wheel Angles (Toe, Caster, And Camber)
  • Adjustment Of Wheel Angles To Meet Manufacturer Specifications
  • Confirmation Of Alignment Correctness Through Final Readings
  • Comprehensive Examination Of Suspension And Steering Systems
  • Assessment Of Shocks And Struts For Signs Of Damage Or Wear
  • Evaluation Of Tire Wear, Tire Condition, And Tire Pressure

If we identify an underlying problem that's causing your vehicle's wheels to go out of alignment, we will skillfully repair or replace damaged or worn components. This ensures that you won't face any further misalignment issues in the future.

Indications And Symptoms Of Wheel Misalignment

In general, it's advisable to have your vehicle's wheel alignment examined every 6,000 miles or every 6 months. However, wheel misalignment problems can crop up between these routine checks. Incidents like minor accidents, encounters with potholes or curbs, and traversing over speed bumps can lead to misaligned wheels. Therefore, if you observe any of the following signs or symptoms, it's important to schedule a vehicle inspection:

  • Excessive Or Uneven Tire Wear
  • A Significant Decrease In Fuel Efficiency
  • The Vehicle Pulling To One Side While Driving
  • Vibrations Or Shaking In The Vehicle
  • The Steering Wheel Not Being Centered When Driving Straight
  • Unusual Sounds Like Squeaking, Creaking, Knocking, Or Rubbing Coming From Underneath The Vehicle

Furthermore, it's highly recommended to have your wheels aligned after fitting a new set of tires. This step ensures that the tires are correctly aligned according to the manufacturer's specifications. Even the slightest deviation from these specifications can result in issues if not rectified. Minor shifts in alignment, regardless of their origin, can have adverse effects on your vehicle's steering, suspension, and tire wear, potentially compromising your safety while driving. Therefore, regular wheel alignment checks and adjustments are crucial for maintaining safe and efficient operation on the road.
