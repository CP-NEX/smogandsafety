Light And Lamp Inspection

Driving without properly functioning lamp equipment is illegal and poses significant safety risks for both the driver and other road users. That's why the DMV mandates a thorough inspection of the vehicle's lighting system by a licensed auto repair expert. At Smog and Safety Inspection, our Lamp Inspection checklist comprises:

  • Headlamps And Tail Lamps: Must Illuminate Correctly With All Adjusting Hardware In Excellent Working Condition.
  • Lenses And Reflectors: Must Be Intact Without Any Cracks.
  • Headlamp Aiming Pads: Should Not Have Any Damage To The Small Points On The Lamp Glass.
  • Visibility: There Should Be No Body Damage That Interferes With The Visibility Of The Lighting Equipment.

If our inspection identifies any lighting equipment that requires repair or replacement, Smog and Safety Inspection provides expert repair services to meet the DMV registration criteria.

Additional Situations Requiring A Brake & Light / Lamp Inspection

While registering a salvaged vehicle is the primary reason for a Brake & Lamp Inspection from a licensed repair shop, there are other situations where the DMV might require or recommend an inspection:

  • Highway Patrol Citation: If A (Highway Patrol) Officer Identifies A Defect On Your Vehicle That Could Pose A Safety Risk Or Create A Hazardous Situation For Other Drivers, You May Receive A Citation Requiring You To Address The Issue Promptly.
  • Purchasing A Used Vehicle: While The DMV Doesn't Mandate A Brake & Lamp Inspection For Used Vehicle Purchases, Many Individuals Opt To Have One To Verify That The Used Car They Intend To Buy Is In Sound Mechanical Condition.
