Quick answers

Brake & Lamp Inspection FAQs

When do I need a brake and lamp inspection?

It is most often required for revived salvage registration, though DMV or CHP follow-up requests may also require inspection documentation.

Can repairs be completed if issues are found?

Yes. If the inspection identifies brake or lighting issues, repair services can be provided before the vehicle proceeds to the next step.

How is this different from a standard brake repair service?

Yes. A brake and lamp inspection is focused on certification and compliance requirements, while brake repair is intended to correct issues such as noise, vibration, or reduced stopping performance.

Need the next step?

Call the shop and explain what your DMV notice, vehicle, or dashboard warning is indicating.
